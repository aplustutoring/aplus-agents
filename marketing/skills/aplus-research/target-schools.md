## How the Research Agent Uses This List

When `aplus-research` runs its weekly Monday morning routine:

### 1. Category C searches (highest priority)
Search for each Tier A partner school by full legal name. Look for:
- Hiring announcements (especially for intervention coordinators, special programs, ELL coordinators)
- Charter renewals or charter petition activity
- Leadership changes (new ED, new principal, new program director)
- Awards, recognitions, or media coverage
- Parent or community complaints in local news
- Board meeting agendas with intervention/funding topics
- Audit findings or compliance issues
- Test score releases (CAASPP, ELPAC, NWEA, MAP)

### 2. Category A (funding) . partner-weighted
When federal/state K-12 funding news surfaces, prioritize stories that affect counties where Tier A partners operate (Sacramento, San Diego, San Bernardino, Riverside, Sonoma, Marin, LA, Ventura).

### 3. Category B (problem stories) . partner-weighted
When LTEL crisis stories, learning loss reports, or achievement gap studies surface, prioritize stories that reference school types matching A+'s partners (independent study charters, NCB charters, virtual academies, Waldorf-inspired charters).

### 4. Geographic clustering
A+ has dense partner clusters in:
- **Sacramento region** (5 partners: Visions, Rio Valley, Heritage, Horizon, plus Tier C overlaps)
- **Sonoma/North Bay** (3 partners: Ocean Grove, Heartwood, plus Forest in Nevada County nearby)
- **San Diego** (4 partners: Heartland, Compass, Pacific Coast Academy, Suncoast Prep)
- **Southern CA inland** (4+ partners: iLEAD, Sage Oak, Gorman, Granite Mountain, Excel, Blueridge)

When news affects multiple partners in a cluster, flag as high-value content opportunity. A single LinkedIn post can leverage multiple partner relationships.

### 5. APLUS+ Network signal
News about the APLUS+ Network, its annual Sacramento conference, or its policy positions = Tier A signal because it touches 10+ partners simultaneously.

## How This List Updates

This list updates when:
- A new partner deal closes (move from Tier C to Tier A, or add new entry)
- An existing partner relationship ends or pauses for 12+ months (move to "former partners" archive)
- A school on Tier C becomes a partner (promote to Tier A)
- A school exits ESSA flagged status (deprioritize in Tier C)
- New schools appear on the CDE NCB list with the right profile (add to Tier C)

**Update cadence:** Quarterly review minimum. Major update annually when CDE ESSA data refreshes.

## Excluded School Types (DO NOT add these)

The research agent should NOT surface these as B2B targets, regardless of how they appear in searches:

- **Self-contained tutoring ecosystems:** Learn4Life family schools, Options for Youth (OFY) all locations, Opportunities for Learning (OFL), SIATech. They sell what A+ sells, no incentive to outsource.
- **WIOA-exclusive schools:** Different funding structure, Title I isn't the lever. Includes Assurance Learning Academy.
- **Credit recovery only / no EL population:** Da Vinci RISE High, MAAC Community Charter, Motivated Youth Academy, Learning Works, Matrix for Success. A+'s LTEL reclassification proof point doesn't transfer.
- **Traditional classroom-based schools:** Even if a school appears on CDE NCB lists in error (like Aspen Valley Prep did). Verify NCB status before adding.
