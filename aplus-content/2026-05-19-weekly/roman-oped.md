I've had three charter directors call me this week with the same question. What should we do with summer? My honest answer: not summer school. Pick the 20 kids who fell behind. Same tutor. 8 weeks. 18 hours. Done.

After 16 years of running this work, I have watched the same thing every May. Spring MAP reports land on the director's desk. The kids who tested below grade level are named in the data. Most of those reports get re-opened in September.

That is the planning failure. Not budget. Not staffing. Reading the report in May and acting on it. The dollars are usually sitting in carryover. The cohort is already named. The only missing piece is somebody deciding before staffing locks in early June.

If you are sitting on a spring MAP report right now, you already know what I am saying.
