Reading is supposed to feel like a door opening. For some kids, it feels like a door that will not budge.

If you have a child in K, 1st, or 2nd grade and reading feels like a fight at the kitchen table, you are not alone, and your instinct to ask questions is right.

Most states now require schools to screen every young child for early signs of dyslexia this fall. If your child is flagged, your school is supposed to have a clear plan to help. Most schools have built the screen. Some have not yet built the plan.

Save this post. Ask your school three questions this summer:

1. Will my child be screened?
2. What happens if my child is flagged?
3. Will the same person work with my child the whole year?
