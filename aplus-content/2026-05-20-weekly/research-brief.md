## Candidate topics (one per category)

### Topic 1 . Category A (Federal procurement / evidence standards) . RECOMMENDED ANCHOR

**Headline:** ESSA's Four Evidence Tiers, And the Question Every Charter Director Should Ask Their Tutoring Vendor

- **Why-now hook:** Spring 2026-27 contract procurement is open at most charter LEAs. Special programs coordinators are reading proposals from 3-8 tutoring vendors in late May and signing by early July. ESSA requires schools using federal funds for intervention to select vendors whose interventions meet Tier 1, Tier 2, or Tier 3 evidence standards. Tier 4 (rationale-only) interventions are usable in narrow cases but should never be the default for a CSI / ATSI improvement plan. Most charter directors do not know the four tiers exist, do not ask which tier the vendor meets, and end up selecting interventions on price + presentation rather than on evidence. The blog walks the four tiers, explains what each requires, and gives the exact single question to ask every vendor before signing a 2026-27 contract.
- **Roman angle:** "Evidence-based" is the most abused phrase in the K-12 vendor pitch deck. Every vendor claims it. Few can back it up. Asking which ESSA tier the intervention meets, and where the evidence lives, ends the bullshit at the first conversation. The vendors who can answer with a citation are the vendors worth talking to.
- **Danielle angle:** A practical guide to the four tiers with examples. Tier 1 (RCT-validated). Tier 2 (quasi-experimental). Tier 3 (correlational outcome evidence). Tier 4 (logic model only). Where most charter tutoring contracts land. iLEAD published case studies as Tier 3 evidence positioned honestly. Walking the charter director through the procurement question: what is your evidence tier, what study supports it, and what is the sample.
- **Target keyword:** ESSA tier 1 2 3 evidence tutoring vendor charter
- **Source URLs:**
  - https://www2.ed.gov/policy/elsec/leg/essa/guidanceuseseinvestment.pdf (USDE ESSA evidence guidance)
  - https://ies.ed.gov/ncee/edlabs/regions/southeast/pdf/ESSA-Tiered-Evidence.pdf (IES regional lab guidance, the most cited primer)
  - https://annenberg.brown.edu/recovery/tutoring (Annenberg Institute high-impact tutoring evidence)
  - https://nssa.stanford.edu/research-database (Stanford NSSA research database for tier-2 RCT examples)
  - https://www2.ed.gov/about/offices/list/oese/oss/technicalassistance/evidenceguidance.pdf (USDE OSS technical assistance)
- **iLEAD evidence fit:** Direct. The published iLEAD case studies (75% Math Tier 3, 87.5% ELA Tier 3, 80% Combined Tier 3) are correlational outcome data with named cohort sizes, defined dosage, and specified intervention conditions. That is exactly what ESSA categorizes as Tier 3 evidence. The blog positions A+ honestly as Tier 3 (not falsely as Tier 1 or Tier 2) and explains why Tier 3 evidence is appropriate for charter LEAs in CSI / ATSI improvement plans.
- **Distinct from May 15, 18, 19:** May 15 was federal funding allocation (Title III). May 18 was state accountability (Dashboard). May 19 was operational programming (summer cohort design). May 20 is procurement / vendor evaluation. No overlap.
- **National applicability:** Strong. ESSA is federal; every state's charter LEAs face the same evidence-tier question in procurement. Nationally-applicable language as required by v1.5.

### Topic 3 . Category C (Multi-state policy)

**Headline:** Dyslexia Screening Laws Hit Implementation Deadlines This Fall. What Charter LEAs Need to Build Before August

- **Why-now hook:** California (SB 114), Texas (HB 3928), Florida (SB 250), Michigan, Connecticut, and 12+ other states have universal dyslexia screening laws with 2025-26 or 2026-27 implementation deadlines. Most charter LEAs have not built the infrastructure to act on a positive screen. Screening without an intervention pathway is malpractice. The blog argues that any charter LEA implementing screening for fall 2026 must also have a Tier 2/3 reading intervention pathway in place before the screen runs.
- **Roman angle:** Screening exists to find struggling readers earlier. Schools that screen without an intervention pathway use the screen as paperwork. The kids stay where they were.
- **Danielle angle:** The pathway from screen-positive to structured-literacy Tier 2 tutoring. Why same-tutor, MAP-aligned, 1:1 fits the dyslexia profile. iLEAD ELA Tier 3 outcomes (87.5%) include students with dyslexia profiles.
- **Target keyword:** dyslexia screening charter school 2026 implementation
- **Source URLs:**
  - https://dyslexiaida.org/state-dyslexia-laws/
  - https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/se/sr/dyslexiaguidelines.asp
  - https://improvingliteracy.org/brief/screening-essentials-students-risk-dyslexia
- **iLEAD evidence fit:** Indirect (adjacent population, not central).

### Topic 4 . Category D (Special programs operational calendar)

**Headline:** The IEP Annual Review Window. Why Charter LEAs Should Use May-June Meetings as an Intervention Audit, Not a Compliance Box

- **Why-now hook:** May and June are when most California charter LEAs run year-end IEP annual reviews. Special programs coordinators are doing dozens of these meetings. Most are treated as IDEA-compliance check-boxes (goals reviewed, service minutes confirmed, signatures collected). The blog argues for using the same meeting cycle as an intervention audit: are the Tier 3 services delivering, what does the spring MAP / CAASPP data show, what should change in the IEP minutes line for next year. The IEP cycle and the Tier 3 cohort design naturally overlap.
- **Roman angle:** IEPs that get signed without anyone looking at outcome data are paperwork. IEPs that get signed after the team reads spring data and adjusts the intervention layer are planning.
- **Danielle angle:** Practical pairing of the IEP meeting and the Tier 3 cohort design conversation. How to bring spring MAP data into the IEP meeting without overloading the team. How the service minutes line should reference the intervention dosage.
- **Target keyword:** IEP annual review charter school intervention audit
- **Source URLs:**
  - https://www2.ed.gov/about/offices/list/osers/osep/index.html (USDE OSEP)
  - https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/se/sr/iepproc.asp (CA IEP process)
  - https://annenberg.brown.edu/recovery/tutoring (intervention dosage evidence)
- **iLEAD evidence fit:** Strong. Many Tier 3 students have active IEPs.
