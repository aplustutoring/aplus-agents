# Facebook Post: Federal Funding Volatility (Parent-Facing B2C)

**Channel:** A+ Tutoring Facebook page
**Voice:** aplus-b2c-brand-kit (warm, direct, parent-relatable)
**Anchor slice from blog:** Section "What works inside a volatile federal funding environment." Translated for parents asking what to ask their child's school.
**Target word count:** 150-250

---

If your child receives any kind of intervention support at school, here's something worth knowing.

Federal K-12 funding has gotten unpredictable this year. Some grants that schools count on for things like extra reading help, English learner support, and after-school programs are being held back by the federal government, even though Congress approved them. The biggest funding sources for schools are still flowing on schedule. Smaller program-specific grants are not.

What this means for families: the intervention services your child is enrolled in this school year are probably safe. The questions worth asking are about next year.

A few things you can ask your child's school over the next few weeks:

Will the small-group reading or math support my child is in this year continue in 2026-27? Are those services funded through the school's main budget, or through a specific federal grant that might be delayed? If a grant is delayed, what is the school's plan?

Schools that have a clear answer to those questions tend to be the ones whose intervention programs hold up year over year. The schools that hesitate are worth a follow-up conversation.

A+ Tutoring works with California charter schools to provide one-on-one and small-group tutoring designed to fit alongside whatever a school's funding mix looks like. If your child's school partners with A+, your special programs coordinator can tell you what's planned for next year.
