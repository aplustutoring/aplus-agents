In my first year teaching I had 32 fourth graders. Ten couldn't read at grade level. We had Title III dollars on the books for kids exactly like them and a Title I budget that had paid for an intervention aide who left in October.

That memory is what I think about when a federal grant gets withheld.

The charter LEAs I work with that protect their Tier 3 program best aren't the ones with the biggest categorical budgets. They're the ones who structured their intervention around a roundtable. School director, special programs coordinator, classroom teacher, parents, A+ tutor. Everyone on the same page about which students, which dose, which subject. The funding mix sits underneath the program, not on top of it.

At iLEAD's combined Tier 3 program last year, 20 students went through Math and ELA cohorts at 17 hours each. 16 improved. That's what the dollars look like when the program is the constant and the funding mix is variable.
