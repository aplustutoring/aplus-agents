If your child is learning English at school or recently arrived in the United States, and your family is part of a California charter school community, here's something worth knowing.

There's federal money called Title III set aside specifically for English learners and newcomer students. Schools receive about $126 per eligible newcomer per year, plus separate formula funding for any student identified as still learning English. That money is meant to pay for things like one-on-one tutoring, small-group instruction, and extra reading and math support during the school day.

The catch: schools have to commit the money by a specific federal deadline (this year, September 30, 2026), or it goes back to the state. A lot of charter schools have unspent balances right now they're working to use before the clock runs out.

What a parent can do this month:

Ask your child's school whether they receive Title III English Learner or Title III Immigrant funding, and what intervention services those dollars support this school year. If your child qualifies and isn't already in a program, ask how to enroll. The funding is meant for kids like yours. They just have to be matched to it.

A+ Tutoring works with charter schools across California to deliver one-on-one and small-group tutoring funded by these federal programs. If your child's school partners with A+, ask the special programs coordinator about a spot.
