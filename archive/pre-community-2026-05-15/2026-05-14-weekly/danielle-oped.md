In my first year teaching, I had 32 fourth graders. Ten couldn't read at grade level. The school had Title III dollars on the books for kids exactly like them. The dollars didn't reach them. Nobody on our small admin team had bandwidth to route the funds, and the year ended.

That memory is why I'm watching the September 30, 2026 Title III deadline so closely this spring.

The charter schools we work with where the dollars actually move have one thing in common: a roundtable. School director, special programs coordinator, classroom teacher, parents, A+ tutor. Everyone on the same page about which students, which dose, which subject.

At iLEAD's Tier 3 program last year, 20 students went through combined Math and ELA cohorts at 17 hours each. 16 of them improved. That's what the dollars look like when they actually reach a student.
