California charter LEAs have until September 30, 2026 to obligate their 2024-25 Title III English Learner and Immigrant funds.

That's 4.5 months from today.

What "obligate" means in federal grant accounting language: enter into a binding contract or commitment that creates a liability against the funds. Setting money aside in a budget line does not count. Verbal agreements do not count. Plans to spend in the next fiscal year do not count.

After September 30, LEAs have 90 days to liquidate. After December 30, 2026, anything still on the books reverts to the California Department of Education.

The pattern CDE's expenditure reports confirm: charter LEAs are statistically more likely than traditional districts to underspend their Title III allocation. Multiple charter LEAs are showing four-to-five-figure unspent Title III balances in CDE's most recent expenditure reports. Smaller business offices, harder vendor identification under the supplemental-not-supplant rule, and audit-risk avoidance all push toward zero spend.

The Title III Immigrant preliminary per-pupil rate for FY 2024-25 is $125.90 (CDE Past Funding Profile ID 6249, https://www.cde.ca.gov/fg/fo/profile.asp?id=6249). For a charter LEA with 50 identified newcomers, that's $6,295 attached to a clock that's already running.

If your school still has Title III dollars on the books, the next 4 weeks are when contracting decisions need to start. Not August. Not Labor Day. Now.
