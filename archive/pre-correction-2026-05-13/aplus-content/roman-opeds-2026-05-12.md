## Op-Ed 1 — Topic 1: "Getting Down to Facts" Report

**Pairs with:** Company Post 1
**Angle:** Founder conviction / system critique
**Word count:** 99

One hundred researchers spent 1,000 pages telling California what charter intervention teams already knew. The interventions that work exist. The state has no mechanism to make them reach the school next door.

This isn't an information problem. It's a political problem dressed up as a complexity problem.

I've watched the same scene for a decade: one school figures out intervention, the school next door reinvents the wheel. Our partners stopped waiting for the state to centralize what works. They went and bought it.

Scaling proven intervention isn't a research finding. It's a decision the state has been avoiding.
