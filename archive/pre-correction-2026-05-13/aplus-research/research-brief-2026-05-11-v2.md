## Summary

Three new May 2026 signals reset this week's agenda. (1) The Trump administration is now holding back roughly **$2 billion in approved K-12 grants**, with **~$1.4B in FY26 funds set to expire in under five months** if OMB doesn't release them. Title I-C, II-A, III-A, IV-A, IV-B remain in limbo. (2) The Trump Education Department has formally moved to **dissolve the Office of English Language Acquisition** (Chalkbeat, April 15), gutting federal EL infrastructure. (3) **FutureEd is tracking 41 state tutoring bills across 19 states in the 2026 session**. Massachusetts proposing $25M for K-3 literacy HIT, Michigan $50M, Connecticut $5M. And California is conspicuously absent. The federal pullback creates uncertainty for every A+ partner that touches Title III; the state policy wave shows what's possible. The Getting Down to Facts report (covered in v1) lands in this exact context.

### Topic 2: Trump Education Department Moves to Dissolve Office of English Learner Acquisition

- **Category:** A (federal policy) + B (LTEL crisis)
- **Source(s):**
  - [Trump Education Department moves to dissolve office serving English learners · Chalkbeat (April 15, 2026)](https://www.chalkbeat.org/2026/04/15/trump-education-department-dissolves-english-learner-office/)
  - [Trump aims to shrink the Education Department · Chalkbeat (March 2026)](https://www.chalkbeat.org/2026/03/03/trump-and-education-department-using-federal-power-for-investigations-and-school-choice/)
  - [Many California students still labeled English learners despite testing as fluent · EdSource](https://edsource.org/2026/california-english-learners-bill/753660)
  - [Creating Coherence: EL Reclassification · Stanford/PACE (March 2026)](https://edpolicyinca.org/sites/default/files/2026-03/r_mercado-garcia_mar2026.pdf)
  - [CDE: English Learners in California Schools (2024-25)](https://www.cde.ca.gov/ds/sg/englishlearner.asp)
- **Why this matters for A+:** OELA is the federal office that has supported state and district EL programs for two decades. Its dissolution doesn't change the count of California's **over 1 million English Learners (17.4% of all CA students, per CDE 2024-25)** or the **~18,500 students annually who score 4 on the ELPAC but aren't reclassified, many of whom drift into LTEL status**. But it removes the federal technical assistance and accountability layer that helped schools fix that pipeline. Translation: California schools are now on their own for EL service delivery, and the ones that already partner with specialist intervention providers are positioned to absorb the gap. Every Tier A partner with EL students is better positioned by this story.
- **Suggested angle for company post:** "The federal Office of English Learner Acquisition is being dismantled. California's 18,500 LTEL students aren't going anywhere. The schools that close the gap will be the ones that don't wait for federal help."
- **Roman take suggestion:** Conviction/system angle. "Federal EL infrastructure is being torn down. The students it served are still here. This is the moment that separates schools that built real intervention from schools that filed compliance paperwork."
- **Danielle take suggestion:** Implementation/teacher angle. "As a former classroom teacher, I knew federal EL support was patchy at best. Now there's no federal layer at all. The schools that built EL intervention with specialist partners (instead of waiting for federal guidance) are the only ones with a real answer for parents in September."

This contrasts directly with high-impact tutoring's robust evidence base (40%+ of schools using HIT, RCT-validated outcomes, 4–10 months of additional learning). LAUSD reviewing all edtech contracts in May 2026 to limit screen time amplifies the narrative. A+ is positioned exactly where the evidence base lives. Human tutors, data-informed, school-day, small-group.
- **Suggested angle for company post:** "Stanford's 2026 review of AI in K-12: 'no high-quality causal studies' in U.S. classrooms. Meanwhile, high-impact tutoring has 40%+ school adoption and replicated effects. The evidence gap isn't subtle."

- **Danielle take suggestion:** Classroom-credibility angle. "Every AI-tutoring vendor pitch I've sat through promises personalization at scale. Stanford's 2026 review says the U.S. K-12 evidence isn't there. The reason is what I've seen in classrooms: students don't learn from chatbots. They learn from a relationship with someone who knows them."

### Topic 5: California MTSS Evaluation Shows Mixed Academic Results. Behavior Gains, ELA Decline

- **Category:** B (problem story) + E (research)
- **Source(s):**
  - [Evaluation of California's Multi-Tiered System of Support shows there's more work to be done · CSBA Blog](https://blog.csba.org/ca-mtss/)
  - [Definition of MTSS · CA Dept of Education](https://www.cde.ca.gov/ci/cr/ri/mtsscomprti2.asp)
  - [How the governor's budget proposal addresses rising numbers of students in special education · EdSource](https://edsource.org/2026/how-the-governors-budget-proposal-addresses-rising-numbers-of-students-in-special-education/750364)
- **Why this matters for A+:** California's statewide MTSS evaluation (covering 2018–19 through 2022–23) shows suspension rates dropped meaningfully. Including a 2.5% decrease for Black students in Phase 2B elementary. But academic outcomes lagged. ELA proficiency fell at most participating secondary schools. Math proficiency dropped 8–10% across phases. The behavioral framework worked; the academic Tier 2 layer didn't. That's the exact gap A+ fills: schools doing MTSS well behaviorally but struggling academically need specialist Tier 2/3 intervention partners. Every Tier A partner doing MTSS gets a story here.
- **Suggested angle for company post:** "California's MTSS evaluation: suspension rates down, ELA proficiency also down. The behavioral framework is working. The academic Tier 2 layer needs reinforcement. And that's where specialist partners earn their place."
- **Roman take suggestion:** System critique. "MTSS was supposed to deliver both behavioral and academic gains. California built the framework; the academic layer never closed. That's not an MTSS failure. That's an intervention-quality failure. You can't build Tier 2 with paraprofessionals and good intentions."
- **Danielle take suggestion:** Implementation angle. "Every special programs coordinator I work with knows MTSS works on paper. The state evaluation confirms what we've seen: behavioral structures hold, academic Tier 2 collapses without trained specialists. The fix isn't more frameworks. It's better Tier 2 delivery."
